## <a name="vectors-and-matrices-in-quantum-computing"></a><span data-ttu-id="0ede8-114">Kuantum bilişiminde vektörler ve matrisler</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-114">Vectors and matrices in quantum computing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-115">[Kuantum bilişimini anlama](xref:microsoft.quantum.overview.understanding) konusunda, bir kubitin 1 veya 0 durumunda ya da bir süper konum veya her ikisinde olduğunu gördünüz.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-115">In the topic [Understanding quantum computing](xref:microsoft.quantum.overview.understanding), you saw that a qubit can be in a state of 1 or 0 or a superposition or both.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-116">Doğrusal cebir kullanılarak bir kubitin durumu, vektör olarak tanımlanır ve tek sütunlu **matris** $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ ile temsil edilir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-116">Using linear algebra, the state of a qubit is described as a vector and is represented by a single column **matrix** $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-117">Bu ayrıca **kuantum durum vektörü** olarak da bilinir ve $|a|^2 + |b|^2 = 1$ gereksinimini karşılamalıdır.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-117">It is also known as a **quantum state vector** and must meet the requirement that $|a|^2 + |b|^2 = 1$.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-120">$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix} \text{ ve }\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{i}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix}.$$</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-120">$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix}, \text{ and }\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\\\  \frac{i}{\sqrt{2}} \end{bmatrix}.$$</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-121">[Kuantum bilgisayarlar ve kuantum simülatörleri](xref:microsoft.quantum.overview.simulators) konusunda, kuantum işlemlerinin bir kubitin durumunu değiştirmek için kullanıldığını da gördünüz.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-121">In [Quantum computers and quantum simulators](xref:microsoft.quantum.overview.simulators) you also saw that quantum operations are used to modify the state of a qubit.</span></span>  <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-122">Kuantum işlemleri bir matris ile de gösterilebilir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-122">Quantum operations can also be represented by a matrix.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-123">Bir kuantum işlemi bir kubite uygulandığında, bunları temsil eden iki matris çarpılır ve elde edilen yanıt, kubitin işlemden sonraki yeni durumunu temsil eder.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-123">When a quantum operation is applied to a qubit, the two matrices that represent them are multiplied and the resulting answer represents the new state of the qubit after the operation.</span></span>  

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-124">Matris çarpımı ile temsil edilen iki yaygın kuantum işlemi aşağıda verilmiştir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-124">Here are two common quantum operations represented with matrix multiplication.</span></span>


<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-125">[`X` işlemi](xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.x), Pauli matrisi $X$,</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-125">The [`X` operation](xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.x) is represented by the Pauli matrix $X$,</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-126">$$X = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\\\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},$$</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-126">$$X = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\\\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},$$</span></span>
    
<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-127">ile temsil edilir ve bir kubitin durumunu 0'dan 1'e (veya tam tersi) çevirmek için kullanılır. Örneğin,</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-127">and is used to flip the state of a qubit from 0 to 1 (or vice-versa), for example</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-128">$$\begin{bmatrix}0 &1\\\\ 1 &0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}.$$</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-128">$$\begin{bmatrix}0 &1\\\\ 1 &0\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}.$$</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-129">['H' işlemi](xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.h), Hadamard dönüştürme $H$,</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-129">The ['H' operation](xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.h) is represented by the Hadamard transformation $H$,</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-130">$$H = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1 &1\\\\ 1 &-1\end{bmatrix},$$</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-130">$$H = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1 &1\\\\ 1 &-1\end{bmatrix},$$</span></span>

 <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-131">ile temsil edilir ve burada gösterildiği gibi, bir kubiti her iki duruma da çökme olasılığı olan bir süper konum durumuna sokar</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-131">and puts a qubit into a superposition state where it has an even probability of collapsing either way, as shown here</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-132">$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1 &1\\\\ 1 &-1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}=\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2=\frac{1}{2}.$$</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-132">$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix}1 &1\\\\ 1 &-1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  0 \end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\\\  1 \end{bmatrix}=\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2=\frac{1}{2}.$$</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-133">Bir kuantum işlemini temsil eden matrisin tek bir gereksinimi vardır: Bir **birim** matris olmalıdır.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-133">A matrix that represents a quantum operation has one requirement – it must be a **unitary** matrix.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-134">Matrisin **tersi**, matrisin **eşlenik devriğine** eşitse bu bir birim matristir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-134">A matrix is unitary if the **inverse** of the matrix is equal to the **conjugate transpose** of the matrix.</span></span>

## <a name="representing-two-qubit-states"></a><span data-ttu-id="0ede8-135">İki kubitli durumları temsil etme</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-135">Representing two-qubit states</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-136">Yukarıdaki örneklerde, bir kubitin durumu, tek sütunlu bir matris olan $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ kullanılarak açıklandı ve buna bir işlemin uygulanması iki matrisin çarpılmasıyla açıklandı.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-136">In the examples above, the state of one qubit was described using a single column matrix $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$, and applying an operation to it was described by multiplying the two matrices.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-137">Ancak kuantum bilgisayarlar birden fazla kubit kullanır, o halde iki kubitin birleşik durumunu nasıl açıklarsınız?</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-137">However, quantum computers use more than one qubit, so how do you describe the combined state of two qubits?</span></span> 

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-138">Her kubitin bir vektör alanı olduğunu ve bu nedenle doğrudan çarpılamayacaklarını unutmayın.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-138">Remember that each qubit is a vector space, so they can't just be multiplied.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-139">Bunun yerine, ayrı ayrı vektör alanlarından yeni bir vektör alanı oluşturan ve $\otimes$ sembolüyle temsil edilen ilgili bir işlem olan **tensör çarpımını** kullanırsınız.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-139">Instead, you use a **tensor product**, which is a related operation that creates a new vector space from individual vector spaces, and is represented by the $\otimes$ symbol.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-140">Örneğin, iki kubit durumunun tensör çarpımı olan $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ ve $\begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix}$ hesaplanır</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-140">For example, the tensor product of two qubit states $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ and $\begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix}$ is calculated</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-141">$$ \begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix} \otimes \begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} a \begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} \\\\ b \begin{bmatrix}c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ac \\\\  ad \\\\  bc \\\\  bd \end{bmatrix}.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-141">$$ \begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix} \otimes \begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} a \begin{bmatrix} c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} \\\\ b \begin{bmatrix}c \\\\  d \end{bmatrix} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ac \\\\  ad \\\\  bc \\\\  bd \end{bmatrix}.</span></span> $$

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-142">Sonuç, her öğenin bir olasılığı temsil ettiği dört boyutlu bir matristir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-142">The result is a four-dimensional matrix, with each element representing a probability.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="0ede8-143">Örneğin, $ac$, iki kubitin 0 ve 0 değerine çökmesi olasılığıdır, $ad$ ise 0 ve 1 olasılığıdır ve bu şekilde devam eder.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-143">For example, $ac$ is the probability of the two qubits collapsing to 0 and 0, $ad$ is the probability of 0 and 1, and so on.</span></span> 

<span data-ttu-id="0ede8-144">Tek bir $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ kubit durumunun bir kuantum durumunu temsil etmesi için $|a|^2 + |b|^2 = 1$ gereksinimini karşılaması gerektiği gibi, $\begin{bmatrix} ac \\\\  ad \\\\  bc \\\\  bd \end{bmatrix}$ iki kubitli durumunun da $|ac|^2 + |ad|^2 + |bc|^2+ |bd|^2 = 1$ gereksinimini karşılaması gerekir.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="0ede8-144">Just as a single qubit state $\begin{bmatrix} a \\\\  b \end{bmatrix}$ must meet the requirement that $|a|^2 + |b|^2 = 1$ in order to represent a quantum state, a two-qubit state $\begin{bmatrix} ac \\\\  ad \\\\  bc \\\\  bd \end{bmatrix}$ must meet the requirement that $|ac|^2 + |ad|^2 + |bc|^2+ |bd|^2 = 1$.</span></span>
